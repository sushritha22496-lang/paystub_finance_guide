HMO vs PPO vs HDHP: Choosing the Right Health Plan (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 7 min read

Employer health insurance comes in several plan types. The main difference: HMOs require a primary care doctor and referrals but have lower premiums; PPOs give flexibility to see any doctor but cost more; HDHPs have high deductibles and low premiums but unlock HSA contributions. During open enrollment, matching the plan to your expected healthcare use can save thousands.

Key Terms You Must Know

TermWhat It Means
PremiumMonthly payment to maintain coverage (deducted from paycheck pre-tax)
DeductibleAmount you pay out-of-pocket before insurance starts sharing costs
CopayFixed fee per visit (e.g., $25/visit); often applies before deductible
CoinsuranceYour share of costs after deductible (e.g., 20% of each bill)
Out-of-Pocket MaximumThe most you pay in a year; after this, insurance pays 100%
In-networkProviders with contracted rates; you pay less for in-network care

The HDHP + HSA Strategy

A High Deductible Health Plan paired with an HSA is one of the most powerful tax-advantaged strategies for healthy workers:

  • Lower premiums free up cash you redirect into an HSA
  • HSA contributions are triple tax-free: deductible going in, grows tax-free, withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free
  • 2024 HSA limits: $4,150 single / $8,300 family (plus $1,000 catch-up if 55+)
  • Unused funds roll over forever—HSA becomes a stealth retirement account (at 65, withdrawals for any purpose taxed as ordinary income, no penalty)

How to Choose

Estimate your annual healthcare costs and compare total out-of-pocket across plans:

  1. Low users (healthy, few visits): HDHP usually wins; lower premium + HSA savings
  2. Moderate users (1–3 specialist visits/year): HMO often best; predictable copays
  3. High users (chronic conditions, frequent care): PPO; out-of-network flexibility and faster specialist access
  4. Families with young children: Consider frequency of pediatric visits; HMO copays often cheaper than PPO coinsurance for frequent visits
💡
Run the math during open enrollment

Add up: annual premium + estimated out-of-pocket (deductible + copays). The plan with the lowest premium isn't always cheapest if you use a lot of healthcare. Most HR portals have plan comparison tools.
