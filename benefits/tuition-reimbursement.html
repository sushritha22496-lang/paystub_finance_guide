Tuition Reimbursement: $5,250 Tax-Free Education Benefit (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

Under IRS Section 127, employers can provide up to $5,250 per year in education assistance completely tax-free to employees. This means no federal income tax, no state income tax, and no FICA on reimbursements up to this amount. Benefits above $5,250 are taxable as wages unless they qualify as a working condition fringe benefit.

What's Covered

Section 127 educational assistance plans can cover:

  • Tuition at accredited colleges and universities
  • Fees, books, supplies, and equipment required for courses
  • Graduate and undergraduate courses (doesn't need to be job-related under Section 127)
  • Certain professional certification programs
  • Student loan repayment — temporarily added through 2025 under the CARES Act; employers can pay up to $5,250/year toward employee student loans tax-free

Not covered: tools kept after course completion, meals, lodging, transportation, courses related to sports/games/hobbies (unless related to employer's business).

Benefits Above $5,250

Reimbursements exceeding $5,250 are taxable wages unless they qualify as a working condition fringe benefit under Section 132. To qualify as a working condition fringe benefit, the education must:

  • Maintain or improve skills required in your current job, OR
  • Be required by law or employer to keep your current job status

The education cannot qualify you for a new career. An attorney studying law qualifies; a dental hygienist studying to become a dentist does not.

Clawback Provisions

Many employers include repayment agreements requiring you to pay back tuition reimbursement if you leave within a certain period (typically 1–2 years). Common structures:

Time After ReimbursementRepayment Required
Leave within 12 months100% repayment
Leave 12–24 months50% repayment
Leave after 24 months0% repayment
💡
Student loan repayment benefit extended through 2025

The CARES Act allowed employers to make tax-free student loan payments of up to $5,250/year on behalf of employees. This provision was extended through December 31, 2025. If your employer offers this, it's one of the best ways to pay down student debt with pre-tax dollars.
