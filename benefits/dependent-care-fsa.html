Dependent Care FSA: $5,000 Tax Break for Childcare (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

A Dependent Care FSA (DCFSA) lets you set aside up to $5,000 per household ($2,500 if married filing separately) in pre-tax dollars to pay for childcare or elder care while you work. The tax savings can be worth $1,000–$2,000 per year depending on your tax bracket.

2024 Contribution Limits

Filing StatusAnnual DCFSA Limit
Single / Married Filing Jointly$5,000 per household
Married Filing Separately$2,500 per spouse

Note: The limit is per household, not per child. If both spouses work and both have access to a DCFSA, combined contributions still can't exceed $5,000.

Eligible Expenses

DCFSA funds can be used for care expenses that allow you and your spouse to work or look for work:

  • Daycare centers and preschool (care component, not education)
  • After-school care programs
  • Day camps (but not overnight camps)
  • In-home babysitters or nannies (must be reported on their taxes)
  • Elder care for a dependent parent or relative who lives with you

Not eligible: overnight camps, kindergarten tuition (education portion), care when a parent is not working, sports/hobby programs.

DCFSA vs. Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit

Both the DCFSA and the Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit (CDCTC) help with care costs, but they interact in an important way:

  • The CDCTC is based on up to $3,000 (1 child) or $6,000 (2+ children) in eligible expenses
  • DCFSA reimbursements reduce the expenses eligible for the CDCTC dollar-for-dollar
  • At a $5,000 DCFSA with 2+ children: $6,000 eligible − $5,000 DCFSA = only $1,000 eligible for CDCTC (worth $200 at 20% credit rate)
  • For most middle-to-high earners, the DCFSA is more valuable due to FICA savings on top of income tax savings

Use-It-or-Lose-It Rule

Like a healthcare FSA, DCFSA funds not used by the plan year deadline (plus any grace period) are forfeited. Unlike an HSA, there is no rollover for DCFSA funds. Plan your contributions carefully based on actual projected care costs.

💡
DCFSA saves on FICA too

Because DCFSA contributions reduce your gross wages, you save not only federal and state income tax but also Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) taxes. On a $5,000 contribution, the FICA savings alone are $382.50—regardless of your income tax bracket.
