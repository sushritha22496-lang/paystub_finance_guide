Open Enrollment: Benefits Checklist & What to Review (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 6 min read

Open enrollment is typically a 2–4 week annual window where you can change your employer benefits elections—health insurance, dental, vision, FSA, HSA, life insurance, disability, and more. Outside this window, you can only change benefits after a qualifying life event. Making uninformed choices can cost thousands over the year.

When Does Open Enrollment Happen?

Most employers hold open enrollment in October or November for coverage starting January 1 of the following year. The ACA Marketplace runs November 1 – January 15 for plans starting January 1. Dates vary—watch for HR communications and set a reminder.

Qualifying Life Events (Outside Open Enrollment)

If you miss open enrollment, you can only change benefits within 30–60 days of a qualifying life event:

  • Marriage or divorce
  • Birth or adoption of a child
  • Loss of other coverage (job loss, spouse loses coverage, aging off parent's plan)
  • Change in employment status (part-time to full-time, or vice versa)
  • Moving to a new coverage area
  • A dependent loses eligibility (turns 26, gets married in some states)

Common Open Enrollment Mistakes

  • Auto-renewing without reviewing — premiums and networks change annually; last year's best plan may not be best today
  • Waiving the FSA/DCFSA — free FICA savings left on the table
  • Under-insuring life insurance — group term may not need evidence of insurability during OE; later increases often require health exam
  • Not checking provider networks — your doctor may be in-network on one plan and out-of-network on another
  • Forgetting beneficiary designations — update life insurance and 401(k) beneficiaries after life events
