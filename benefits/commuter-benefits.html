Commuter Benefits: Pre-Tax Transit & Parking (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 4 min read

Employer commuter benefits let you pay for transit passes and qualified parking with pre-tax payroll deductions. In 2024, you can exclude up to $315 per month for transit/vanpool and $315 per month for qualified parking from your taxable income—a combined potential savings of $630/month tax-free.

How Much Can You Save?

Tax savings depend on your marginal rate. Example for someone in the 22% federal bracket using both $315/month transit and $315/month parking:

  • Annual pre-tax benefit: $315 × 12 × 2 = $7,560/year
  • Federal income tax savings (22%): $1,663
  • FICA savings (7.65%): $578
  • State tax savings (est. 5%): $378
  • Total annual savings: ~$2,619

How to Enroll

Most employers offer commuter benefits through a benefits portal or dedicated transit benefit provider (e.g., WageWorks, Commuter Benefits Solutions, or Edenred). Enrollment is typically year-round (not limited to open enrollment). You designate a monthly amount that's deducted from each paycheck pre-tax and loaded onto a transit card or parking account.

💡
Different from a Healthcare FSA

Unlike a healthcare FSA, unused commuter benefit funds typically roll over month-to-month within the same employer (no use-it-or-lose-it). However, unused funds are generally forfeited when you leave the employer. Adjust your monthly election to match what you'll actually spend.
