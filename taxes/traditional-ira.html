Traditional IRA: Contribution Limits, Deductibility & Rules (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 6 min read

A Traditional IRA lets you contribute up to $7,000 per year ($8,000 if age 50+) in 2024. Contributions may be tax-deductible depending on your income and whether you or your spouse have a workplace retirement plan. Growth is tax-deferred; withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.

2024 Contribution Limits

AgeAnnual Contribution LimitDeadline
Under 50$7,000Tax filing deadline (April 15, 2025)

You can contribute to both a Traditional and Roth IRA, but the combined total cannot exceed $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+). Contributions must be from earned income (wages, self-employment)—not investment income.

Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

Starting at age 73 (under SECURE 2.0), you must take annual RMDs from your Traditional IRA. The amount is calculated by dividing your account balance (Dec 31 prior year) by the IRS life expectancy factor for your age. Failure to take RMDs results in a 25% excise tax on the amount not withdrawn (reduced to 10% if corrected promptly).

💡
Non-deductible contributions: track with Form 8606

If you make non-deductible Traditional IRA contributions (over income limits), file Form 8606 each year. This establishes your "basis" so you're not taxed twice on withdrawals. Missing 8606 filings can result in paying tax on money you already paid tax on.
