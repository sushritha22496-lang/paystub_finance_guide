Capital Gains Tax Rates 2024: Short-Term vs Long-Term

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 6 min read

Capital gains tax is the tax you pay when you sell an asset for more than you paid for it. Short-term gains (assets held 1 year or less) are taxed as ordinary income. Long-term gains (held over 1 year) are taxed at preferential rates of 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on your income.

Short-Term Capital Gains

If you sell an asset you've held for one year or less, the profit is a short-term capital gain. It's added to your ordinary income and taxed at your regular marginal rate—up to 37%.

💡
Holding just one extra day can save thousands

If you're close to the one-year mark on a profitable investment, consider waiting before selling. Crossing from short-term to long-term treatment can drop your rate by 17–20 percentage points.

2024 Long-Term Capital Gains Rates

Long-term rates are significantly lower than ordinary income rates. For 2024:

Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)

High earners face an additional 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax on top of the capital gains rate. NIIT applies to the lesser of your net investment income or the amount your MAGI exceeds:

  • $200,000 for single filers
  • $250,000 for married filing jointly
  • $125,000 for married filing separately

This means the top effective rate on long-term gains can reach 23.8% (20% + 3.8% NIIT) for the highest earners.

Tax-Loss Harvesting

Tax-loss harvesting is the strategy of selling investments at a loss to offset capital gains and reduce your tax bill:

  1. Offset gains dollar-for-dollar — capital losses reduce capital gains of the same type first
  2. Offset up to $3,000 of ordinary income — if losses exceed gains, you can deduct up to $3,000/year against wages
  3. Carry forward excess losses — losses beyond $3,000 carry forward indefinitely to future years
⚠️
Wash-sale rule

You cannot buy the same or a "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after a loss sale. Doing so disallows the loss. You can buy a similar (but not identical) security to maintain market exposure.
