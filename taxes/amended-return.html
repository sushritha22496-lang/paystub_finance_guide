How to Amend a Tax Return (Form 1040-X)

Updated June 2026 • 9 min read
Quick Answer: File Form 1040-X within 3 years of the original filing deadline (or 2 years from when you paid) to claim a refund. If you owe more tax, file and pay as soon as possible to minimize interest.

When Should You Amend?

You should file an amended return when you discover errors that change your tax liability. Common reasons include:

  • Received a corrected W-2 or 1099 after filing
  • Forgot to claim a deduction or credit (mortgage interest, education credit, charitable contributions)
  • Incorrectly reported income or filing status
  • Did not claim all dependents you were entitled to claim
  • IRS corrected your math errors but you disagree with the result

Do not amend for simple math errors — the IRS corrects those automatically and will notify you if changes affect your liability.

The 3-Year / 2-Year Deadline

To receive a refund from an amended return, you must file within:

  • 3 years from the date you filed the original return, OR
  • 2 years from the date you paid the tax — whichever is later

If you filed your 2021 return on April 15, 2022, you have until April 15, 2025 to file a 1040-X and claim a refund. Returns filed after the deadline still amend the record, but any refund is forfeited.

If you owe additional tax, there is no time limit — but interest accrues from the original due date, so amend promptly.

How to Complete Form 1040-X

Form 1040-X has three columns:

ColumnWhat to Enter
Column AOriginal figures as previously reported (or as last amended)
Column BNet change — increase or (decrease)
Column CCorrect figures (Column A + Column B)

Part III requires a written explanation of what changed and why. Be specific — for example: “Received corrected 1099-DIV from Vanguard showing $1,450 in qualified dividends, not reported on original return.”

Documents to Attach

Attach any forms or schedules that changed. For example:

  • The corrected 1099 or W-2
  • Schedule A if you are now itemizing
  • Form 8863 if you are adding an education credit
  • Do NOT re-attach forms that did not change

Can You E-File an Amended Return?

Since 2021, the IRS allows e-filing of Form 1040-X for tax years 2019 and later through most major tax software. E-filing is faster and reduces processing errors. Check your software's support page — some platforms still require paper for complex amendments.

Paper 1040-X returns are mailed to a specific IRS address depending on your state. The current addresses are listed in the Form 1040-X instructions at IRS.gov.

Processing Time

Amended returns take significantly longer than original returns to process:

  • E-filed: 16–20 weeks typically
  • Paper filed: 20+ weeks — often longer during peak periods

Track your amended return status at IRS.gov using “Where's My Amended Return?” (available 3 weeks after mailing or 24 hours after e-filing).

State Amended Returns

If your federal amendment changes your state tax liability, you must also file an amended state return. Most states have their own equivalent form (e.g., California Form 540X). State deadlines vary — generally 4 years from original filing in most states.
