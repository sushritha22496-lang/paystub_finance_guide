Estimated Quarterly Taxes: How to Pay & Avoid Penalties (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 6 min read

If you expect to owe at least $1,000 in federal taxes after withholding and credits, you generally must pay estimated taxes quarterly. This applies to freelancers, self-employed workers, investors with capital gains, landlords, and employees with significant income outside their paycheck.

2024 Estimated Tax Due Dates

Payment PeriodIncome DatesDue Date
Q1 2024Jan 1 – Mar 31April 15, 2024
Q2 2024Apr 1 – May 31June 17, 2024
Q3 2024Jun 1 – Aug 31September 16, 2024
Q4 2024Sep 1 – Dec 31January 15, 2025
⚠️
The quarters are unequal

Q1 covers 3 months, Q2 covers 2 months, Q3 covers 3 months, and Q4 covers 4 months. This is counterintuitive but is how the IRS defines the periods. Set calendar reminders for each due date.

Safe Harbor Rules (Avoid Penalties)

You avoid underpayment penalties by meeting one of these safe harbor thresholds:

Safe Harbor MethodRule
90% of current year taxTotal payments cover 90% of what you'll owe for 2024
100% of prior year taxPay 100% of your 2023 total tax liability (easiest if income varies)
110% of prior year taxRequired if 2023 AGI exceeded $150,000 (MFJ) / $75,000 (MFS)

Most self-employed people use the prior year method: divide last year's tax by 4 and pay that each quarter. Eliminates guesswork about current-year income.

How to Calculate Your Payment

  1. Estimate annual net income — project revenues minus business expenses
  2. Calculate SE tax — 15.3% on 92.35% of net SE income
  3. Subtract half of SE tax — reduces AGI
  4. Apply standard deduction — $14,600 single / $29,200 MFJ in 2024
  5. Calculate federal income tax — using tax brackets on taxable income
  6. Add SE tax + income tax — total tax owed
  7. Subtract any withholding — from a W-2 job if you have one
  8. Divide by 4 — for equal quarterly payments

How to Make Payments

  • IRS Direct Pay — free, direct bank debit at IRS.gov/payments
  • EFTPS — Electronic Federal Tax Payment System; schedule future payments in advance
  • IRS2Go app — mobile payment option
  • Mail Form 1040-ES — with a check to the IRS (include your SSN and tax year)
  • Credit/debit card — via authorized third-party processors (processing fees apply: ~1.85–1.98%)
