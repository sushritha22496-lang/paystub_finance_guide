Minimum Wage by State 2024: All 50 States + Federal Rate

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

The federal minimum wage is $7.25/hour — unchanged since 2009. But 30+ states have higher minimum wages, and employers must pay whichever is higher. Several major states are at $16/hour or above in 2024, and the trend toward $15 as a national floor continues.

Cities can set their own (higher) minimum wages

Many cities exceed their state minimum: Seattle $19.97/hr, San Francisco $18.67/hr, Denver $18.29/hr, Chicago $15.80/hr. Employers must pay the highest applicable rate: federal, state, or local.

Tipped Minimum Wage

Under federal law, employers can pay tipped employees as little as $2.13/hour in direct wages — but total pay (wages + tips) must always equal at least $7.25/hour. If tips don't make up the difference, the employer must pay the gap.

However, many states prohibit the tip credit and require all employees to receive the full state minimum wage before tips. These include California, Oregon, Washington, Minnesota, Alaska, and others.

Youth / Training Minimum Wage

Federal law allows employers to pay workers under age 20 just $4.25/hour for the first 90 calendar days of employment. After 90 days or once the employee turns 20, the full minimum wage applies. Many states do not allow this youth wage.
