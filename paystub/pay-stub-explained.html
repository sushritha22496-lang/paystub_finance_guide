How to Read a Pay Stub: Every Field Explained

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 6 min read

Your pay stub is a detailed record of your earnings and deductions for a pay period. It shows gross pay (what you earned), all taxes and deductions taken out, and net pay (what you take home). The YTD (year-to-date) column tracks running totals. Understanding every line helps you verify accuracy and make smarter financial decisions.

Net Pay: The Bottom Line

Net Pay = Gross Pay − All Taxes − All Deductions

In our example: $2,807.69 − $677.10 (taxes) − $565.38 (deductions) = $1,565.21 net pay

✅
Always verify your pay stub

Check for errors in hours worked, pay rate, and deduction amounts every pay period. Payroll mistakes are more common than you think, and it's easier to correct them early than after year-end W-2s are issued.
