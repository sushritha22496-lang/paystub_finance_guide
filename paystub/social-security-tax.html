Social Security Tax: Rate, Wage Base & How It Works (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

Social Security tax (labeled OASDI or SS on your pay stub) is 6.2% of your wages, matched by your employer for a combined 12.4%. In 2024, only the first $168,600 of earnings is subject to this tax—earnings above that are exempt. This cap means high earners stop paying Social Security tax partway through the year.

The Wage Base Cap

The Social Security wage base ($168,600 in 2024) is adjusted annually for inflation. Once your cumulative earnings reach this threshold in any calendar year, Social Security withholding stops for the rest of the year. Your pay stub YTD column will show this clearly.

What Does Social Security Tax Fund?

OASDI stands for Old-Age, Survivors, and Disability Insurance. The 12.4% combined rate is split:

  • 10.6% goes to the Old-Age and Survivors Insurance (OASI) Trust Fund — funds retirement and survivor benefits
  • 1.8% goes to the Disability Insurance (DI) Trust Fund — funds SSDI benefits

Work Credits for Future Benefits

Every dollar of Social Security tax you pay builds your future benefit. In 2024, you earn 1 work credit per $1,730 in wages (up to 4 credits per year). You need 40 credits (10 years of work) to qualify for retirement benefits. Your benefit amount is calculated from your highest 35 years of indexed earnings.
