Pay Stub Generator: What to Look For and How They Work

Updated June 2026 • 8 min read
Quick Answer: Legitimate pay stub generators are used by self-employed workers and small businesses to document income. They require accurate inputs — the output is only as reliable as the data you enter. Fabricating stubs for loans or rentals is illegal.

Who Uses Pay Stub Generators?

Pay stub generators produce standardized pay stub documents from inputs you provide. They serve several legitimate purposes:

  • Self-employed / freelancers: Sole proprietors and independent contractors do not receive employer-issued pay stubs. A generator lets them document income for loan applications, apartment rentals, or proof-of-income letters.
  • Small business owners: Very small businesses without payroll software can use generators to provide stubs to employees, though dedicated payroll software (Gusto, QuickBooks Payroll) is more reliable.
  • Gig economy workers: Uber, DoorDash, and similar platforms provide earnings statements but not traditional pay stubs. A generator can format this data in a recognizable layout.

How to Verify a Pay Stub's Accuracy

Whether you generated your own stub or received one from an employer, verify these calculations:

  1. Social Security tax: Should be exactly 6.2% of gross wages (up to $168,600 for 2024)
  2. Medicare tax: Should be exactly 1.45% of gross wages (no cap)
  3. Federal withholding: Should match IRS withholding tables for your filing status and pay frequency
  4. Net pay: Gross − all deductions and taxes should equal the net amount shown
  5. YTD totals: Should match the sum of all previous pay periods

If any of these fail to match, the stub may contain errors — either from incorrect inputs or a poorly designed generator.

Red Flags on a Received Pay Stub

If you are a landlord, lender, or employer reviewing a submitted pay stub, watch for:

  • FICA taxes that do not equal exactly 6.2% / 1.45% of gross pay
  • Round-number deductions with no explanation
  • YTD totals inconsistent with the pay period sequence (e.g., period 6 of 26 with oddly high YTD)
  • Font inconsistencies or formatting that differs mid-document
  • Missing employer EIN (Employer Identification Number)
  • State withholding for a state where the employer does not operate

Legal Considerations

Using a pay stub generator for legitimate documentation of your actual income is legal. Using one to falsify income for a loan application, rental agreement, or government program constitutes fraud and can result in:

  • Criminal charges (wire fraud, bank fraud — federal felonies)
  • Civil liability to the lender or landlord
  • Eviction or loan acceleration if discovered after the fact

Better Alternatives for Self-Employed Income Verification

Lenders and landlords increasingly accept these alternatives to pay stubs:

  • Two years of signed federal tax returns (Schedule C for sole proprietors)
  • Bank statements showing consistent deposits (12–24 months)
  • 1099 forms from clients
  • A CPA-prepared profit-and-loss statement
  • Business bank statements separate from personal accounts
