Salary vs. Hourly: Pros, Cons & Key Differences

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

Salaried employees receive a fixed annual pay divided into equal paychecks, regardless of hours worked. Hourly employees are paid for each hour worked — and must receive overtime for hours over 40/week. Each structure has distinct advantages depending on your career goals, industry, and work style.

Converting Between Salary and Hourly

To compare job offers with different pay structures:

  • Salary to hourly: Annual salary ÷ 2,080 hours (52 weeks × 40 hrs)
  • Hourly to annual: Hourly rate × 2,080 hours

The overtime trap: salaried isn't always better

If you work significant overtime in an hourly role, your effective compensation can far exceed a comparable salary. A $50k salary is $24/hr — but if you regularly work 50 hours/week in an hourly job at $20/hr, you earn $1,100/week = $57,200/year effective.

Which Is Better for You?

  • You value income predictability for budgeting
  • You want comprehensive benefits (health, 401k, PTO)
  • You're in a management or professional career track
  • You prefer flexibility in daily schedule/hours
  • You want to be paid for every hour you work
  • You work significant overtime and want to be compensated for it
  • You want clear separation between work and personal time
  • You're in a field that typically pays well hourly (trades, healthcare, tech contracting)
