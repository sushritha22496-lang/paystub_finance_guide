How Are Bonuses Taxed? Supplemental Wage Rate Explained (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

Bonuses are classified as supplemental wages by the IRS. Federal income tax is withheld at a flat 22% for amounts up to $1 million, and 37% for amounts exceeding $1 million. You also owe Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) on top. Your actual tax rate on the bonus depends on your total income for the year—the 22% withholding is just an estimate.

Two Withholding Methods

Employers can use one of two IRS-approved methods to withhold on bonuses:

MethodHow It WorksWhen Used
Percentage (Flat Rate)Withhold 22% flat federal tax on the bonus amountMost common; bonus paid separately from regular wages
Aggregate MethodAdd bonus to regular wages, calculate tax on combined total, subtract already-withheld taxUsed when bonus combined with regular paycheck

Example — Percentage Method: $5,000 bonus × 22% federal = $1,100 withheld. Plus 6.2% SS ($310) + 1.45% Medicare ($72.50) = $1,482.50 total withheld. You receive ~$3,517.

Your True Bonus Tax Rate

The 22% withholding is just a withholding estimate—not your final tax rate. At tax filing time, your bonus is simply added to all other income and taxed at your marginal rate. If your marginal rate is lower than 22%, you may get a refund. If higher, you'll owe more.

Your Marginal RateWithholding RateResult at Filing

22%22%Even — no change

Strategies to Reduce Bonus Tax

  • Max out 401(k) — contribute bonus dollars to a traditional 401(k); reduces your taxable income dollar-for-dollar (up to the $23,000 annual limit in 2024)
  • Contribute to HSA — if on an HDHP, contribute up to $4,150 (single) / $8,300 (family) pre-tax
  • Defer the bonus — if possible, ask employer to pay the bonus in January of the next year to push it into a new tax year
  • Charitable bunching — donate appreciated stock or lump charitable contributions into the bonus year to itemize
💡
State taxes apply too

Many states also withhold state income tax on bonuses. Some use a flat supplemental rate; others add it to regular wages. Check your state's rules—bonus withholding ranges from 0% (no income tax states like TX, FL) to over 13% in California.
