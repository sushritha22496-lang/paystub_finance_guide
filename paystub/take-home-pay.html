How to Calculate Your Take-Home Pay (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 6 min read

Take-home pay (net pay) is what you actually receive after all taxes and deductions. For most workers, take-home is roughly 65–75% of gross pay. The exact amount depends on your income, filing status, pre-tax deductions, and state. Here's how to calculate it step by step.

The Take-Home Pay Formula

Take-Home Pay = Gross Pay − Pre-Tax Deductions − Federal Income Tax − State Income Tax − Social Security Tax − Medicare Tax − Post-Tax Deductions

Step-by-Step Example

Single filer, $60,000/year salary, bi-weekly pay (26 periods), standard W-4, contributing $200/paycheck to 401(k) and $50 to health insurance premiums:

StepItemPer Paycheck
1Gross pay ($60,000 ÷ 26)$2,307.69
2− 401(k) pre-tax contribution−$200.00
3− Health insurance premium (pre-tax)−$50.00
4= Federal taxable wages$2,057.69
5− Federal income tax withheld (est. ~12% effective)−$247.00
6− Social Security (6.2% of $2,307.69)−$143.08
7− Medicare (1.45% of $2,307.69)−$33.46
8− State income tax (est. ~4% for mid-tax state)−$82.31
✔= Take-Home Pay$1,801.84

Effective take-home rate: ~78% of gross. Annual take-home: ~$46,848.

How to Maximize Take-Home Pay

  • Maximize pre-tax deductions — every $1 in 401(k), HSA, or FSA reduces your taxable wages and lowers withholding
  • Update your W-4 — claiming the right allowances prevents over-withholding; use the IRS Tax Withholding Estimator
  • Use a commuter benefit — up to $315/month pre-tax for transit/parking saves FICA and income taxes
  • Check dependent care FSA — up to $5,000/year pre-tax for childcare reduces taxable income
💡
Pre-tax deductions reduce FICA too

401(k) and HSA contributions reduce federal and state income tax, but they also reduce Social Security and Medicare taxes since they lower your gross wages for FICA purposes. A $200 pre-tax 401(k) contribution saves you about $15.30 in FICA taxes alone.
