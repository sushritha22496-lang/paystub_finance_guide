Paycheck Calculator: How to Calculate Your Take-Home Pay

Updated June 2026 • 10 min read
Quick Answer: Your net pay = gross pay − pre-tax deductions − federal income tax − state income tax − Social Security (6.2%) − Medicare (1.45%) − post-tax deductions.

The Paycheck Formula

Every paycheck follows the same basic sequence of calculations. Understanding each step lets you verify your stub and plan your finances accurately.

  1. Start with gross pay (salary ÷ pay periods, or hours × rate)
  2. Subtract pre-tax deductions (401k, HSA, health insurance, FSA)
  3. Calculate FICA taxes on the gross amount
  4. Calculate federal income tax withholding on taxable wages
  5. Calculate state income tax if applicable
  6. Subtract post-tax deductions (Roth 401k, life insurance, garnishments)
  7. Result = net pay

Assumptions: Single, 1 allowance, bi-weekly pay (26 periods), $200 traditional 401k/period, $50 health insurance/period, Texas resident (no state income tax).

Federal Income Tax Withholding

Federal withholding is calculated using IRS Publication 15-T tables or the percentage method. The amount depends on:

  • Your W-4 filing status and extra withholding instructions
  • Whether you claimed exemption from withholding
  • Any Step 4 additional withholding amounts you requested

Since 2020, the new W-4 no longer uses allowances. Instead it uses income amounts, deductions, and credits to determine withholding. Update your W-4 whenever you have a major life change (marriage, child, second job).

Tips to Increase Your Paycheck

  • Review your W-4: If you consistently get a large refund, you are over-withholding. Adjust Step 3 or 4 to keep more money each pay period.
  • Maximize HSA contributions: HSA dollars reduce FICA in addition to income tax — a double benefit traditional 401k doesn't provide.
  • Enroll in FSA for known expenses: Medical and dependent care FSA reduce taxable wages.
  • Live in a no-income-tax state: TX, FL, NV, WA, SD, WY, TN, and NH have no broad-based income tax on wages.
