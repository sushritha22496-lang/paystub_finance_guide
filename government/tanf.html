What Is TANF? Cash Assistance Eligibility & Benefits (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

TANF (Temporary Assistance for Needy Families) is a federal block grant program that provides cash assistance and work support to low-income families with children. Unlike many federal programs, TANF gives states significant flexibility in how they design their programs, so eligibility rules, benefit amounts, and available services vary widely by state.

General Eligibility

TANF is generally available to:

  • Families with children under 18 (or 19 if full-time students)
  • Very low income—specific limits set by each state
  • U.S. citizens and certain qualified immigrants
  • Adults who cooperate with child support enforcement
  • Adults who meet work requirements (or are exempt due to disability, caring for infant, etc.)

The 60-Month Lifetime Limit

Federal law imposes a 60-month (5-year) lifetime limit on TANF cash assistance using federal funds. Key points:

  • The 60 months don't have to be consecutive
  • Some states have shorter limits (e.g., Indiana: 24 months; Arkansas: 24 months)
  • Some states use state-only funds to serve families past the federal limit
  • The limit applies to adults; children are not time-limited

Work Requirements

TANF recipients generally must participate in work activities within 24 months of receiving benefits. Qualifying activities include:

  • Unsubsidized or subsidized employment
  • Job search and job readiness assistance (limited weeks)
  • Work experience or community service
  • Vocational educational training (limited to 12 months)
  • On-the-job training
💡
TANF covers more than cash

States use TANF funds for a broad range of services beyond cash: childcare subsidies, job training, transportation assistance, pregnancy prevention programs, and more. Contact your local social services office to learn what your state offers beyond monthly grants.
