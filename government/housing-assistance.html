Housing Assistance: Section 8, Public Housing & HUD Programs (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 6 min read

Federal housing assistance helps low-income families, the elderly, and people with disabilities afford safe housing. The two main programs are the Housing Choice Voucher Program (Section 8)—which subsidizes rent in private housing—and Public Housing—government-owned apartment complexes with reduced rents. Both are administered by local Public Housing Authorities (PHAs) and have long waitlists.

Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers

Section 8 vouchers subsidize rent in private apartments. How it works:

  1. You pay 30% of your adjusted monthly income toward rent and utilities
  2. The voucher covers the rest, paid directly to the landlord, up to the area's Payment Standard (based on Fair Market Rents)
  3. You choose your own unit from willing landlords in the private market
  4. Voucher is portable — in most cases, you can move to another area after one year

Eligibility

CriteriaRequirement
IncomeGenerally below 50% of Area Median Income (AMI); 75% of new vouchers must go to households below 30% AMI
CitizenshipU.S. citizen or qualifying immigration status for at least one family member
BackgroundPHAs may deny based on prior evictions from assisted housing or certain criminal history
Family compositionIndividuals, families, elderly (62+), and people with disabilities all qualify

The Waitlist Reality

Demand far exceeds supply. Key facts about waitlists:

  • Most PHAs have waitlists of 1–10+ years
  • Many PHAs close their waitlists entirely when they can't process new applicants
  • Approximately 5 million households receive housing vouchers; millions more are on waitlists
  • Strategy: Apply to multiple PHAs simultaneously; waitlists open and close unpredictably; apply immediately when a waitlist opens

Other HUD Programs

  • Public Housing — government-owned units; rent = 30% of adjusted income; apply directly to local PHA
  • Section 8 Project-Based Rental Assistance — subsidy attached to specific units; apply at the property
  • HOME Investment Partnerships — federal grants to states for affordable housing development
  • Emergency Rental Assistance — state/local programs; availability varies; search HUD.gov for current programs
💡
Apply to every open waitlist near you

Search HUD's PHA directory at HUD.gov/program_offices/public_indian_housing/pha/contacts to find every housing authority in your area. Sign up for waitlist notification services. Apply the day a waitlist opens—waitlist positions are typically assigned by lottery or date of application.
