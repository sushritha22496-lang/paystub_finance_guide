What Is WIC? Eligibility, Benefits & How to Apply (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 5 min read

WIC (Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants, and Children) is a federal program serving about 6.7 million people per month. It provides nutritious foods, nutrition education, breastfeeding support, and healthcare referrals to low-income pregnant women, postpartum women, breastfeeding women, infants, and children up to age 5.

Who Is Eligible?

To qualify for WIC, you must meet ALL of these criteria:

  1. Category: Be pregnant, postpartum (up to 6 months after delivery), breastfeeding (up to 12 months), an infant (under 1), or a child under age 5
  2. Income: Household income at or below 185% of the Federal Poverty Level
  3. Nutritional risk: Determined by a health professional (most applicants qualify)

What WIC Provides

  • Food benefits — monthly food package or eWIC card for specific nutritious foods (milk, eggs, whole grains, fruits/vegetables, legumes, infant formula, cereal)
  • Nutrition education — counseling and classes on healthy eating and cooking
  • Breastfeeding support — peer counselors, lactation support, breast pumps
  • Healthcare referrals — connections to prenatal care, well-child visits, immunizations, dental care

How to Apply

  1. Find your local WIC agency at WIC.fns.usda.gov or call 1-800-942-3678
  2. Schedule an appointment (many states now offer telehealth appointments)
  3. Bring: proof of identity, proof of residency, income documentation, and proof of pregnancy or child's age
  4. Attend a brief nutrition screening appointment
  5. If approved, receive your eWIC card or food vouchers
💡
WIC is not SNAP

WIC and SNAP (food stamps) are separate programs with different income limits and eligible foods. You can receive both simultaneously. WIC covers specific nutritious foods for targeted populations; SNAP covers a broader range of foods for low-income households of any composition. If you qualify for WIC, also check SNAP eligibility.
