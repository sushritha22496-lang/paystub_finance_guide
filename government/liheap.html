LIHEAP: Home Energy Assistance Program Eligibility (2024)

📅 Updated 2024⏱ 4 min read

LIHEAP (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program) is a federally funded program that helps low-income households pay for heating and cooling costs. It's administered by states and territories, so eligibility rules, benefit amounts, and application periods vary. About 6 million households receive LIHEAP assistance annually.

Who Qualifies?

Most states set income limits between 60% of State Median Income or 150% of the Federal Poverty Level (whichever is higher). Priority is given to households with:

  • Elderly members (60+)
  • People with disabilities
  • Young children (under 5)
  • Households in immediate crisis (heating/cooling shutoff, no fuel)

What LIHEAP Covers

  • Heating assistance — help paying winter utility bills (gas, electric, oil, propane, wood)
  • Cooling assistance — help with summer electric bills or air conditioner purchase
  • Energy crisis assistance — emergency help to restore heat/cooling when service is cut off
  • Weatherization — some states use LIHEAP funds for home energy efficiency improvements (insulation, weatherstripping)

How to Apply

  1. Contact your state or local LIHEAP office — find yours at benefits.gov or call 1-866-674-6327
  2. Apply during your state's open application period (usually fall/winter for heating)
  3. Bring: proof of income, utility bills, Social Security numbers for all household members, proof of address
  4. Approved benefits are typically paid directly to your utility company or fuel supplier
💡
Apply early—funds run out

LIHEAP is funded by annual congressional appropriations. Many states exhaust their funds before the end of heating season. Apply as early as your state's application window opens. If you're in crisis (no heat, shutoff notice), ask specifically about emergency LIHEAP assistance—crisis funds are separate and may be available when regular funds are exhausted.
